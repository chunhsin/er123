About E.R.

Come and tell us your stories!


Fawn Chang, the founder of E.R. Counseling Center in Taipei, Taiwan, boasts an illustrious career spanning over 25 years in the counseling field.

Her and her team’s focus primarily revolve around Adlerian Psychology, which they utilize in their work with families, couples, children, and individuals.

The acronym ‘E.R.’ stands for the core approach of Adlerian Psychology known as ‘Early Recollection.’

At E. R. Counseling Center, the counselors employ the technique of delving into their clients’ childhood memories through the Early Recollection method.

By doing so, they help their clients to gain valuable insights into their lifestyles and self-concepts.

Furthermore, this process aids clients in understanding how their worldviews and life patterns are formed.

Armed with this newfound understanding, the team guides their clients towards rebuilding healthier perceptions and behavioral patterns.

Consequently, the clients are empowered with the courage to embark on transformative journeys, making positive and impactful changes in their lives.

Process

Counseling procedure

Q：I have never done counseling before. What is it like? Do I really need counseling?

A：We encourage you to call and discuss your concerns before making an appointment. The below procedure can also give you an heads-up. (Please click on the arrows for more information.）


Step1. Request >>>

There will be a 5-10 minute phone interview to understand the area(s) of your issues. We will help you find the counselor that would better suit you. Your demand in terms of appointment logs and number of sessions will be arranged accordingly. An approx. 10 min of administrative time (filling forms, counseling process, confidentiality) will not be calculated in the counseling fee.

<<< Step2. Counseling>>>

The counselor will work with you to find possible cause(s) to your concerns. A recollection of past events may be reviewed to reflect on the current challenges. You will be working toward the preparation to face future challenges.

<<< Step3. Closure>>>

When the therapeutic goal is reached, the counselor will help you with the termination process. This includes reviewing your milestones, strengthening learned skills, and discussing the necessity of a potential assessment. A recommendation to the frequency of the future meeting(s) will also be discussed.

<<< Other. Referrals

We will provide referral information when there is a need to do so. The counselor will walk you through the process and help you find the most suitable and reliable resource(s) to meet your needs.
